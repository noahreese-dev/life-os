What Happened

The cost problem nobody was watching

The morning briefing went dark two days ago. Task Scheduler showed result 0. The log said "completed successfully." But the CEO got nothing. Silent failure — the worst kind.

The root cause was embarrassingly simple: every autonomous script was running on Opus, the most expensive model. A morning briefing — summarize STATUS.md, list today's contract — was burning through a $1.00 budget in 4 minutes flat. The error message ("Exceeded USD budget") was 37 characters long, just enough to slip past the 20-character fallback check. No briefing. No Telegram. No trace.

Fix #57 caught it for the morning briefing. Fix #58 extends the same logic to all 6 routine scripts: nightly accountability, deploy check, sleep nudge, phone-free morning, and weekly digest. All now route to Sonnet — 5-10x cheaper per token, more than capable for these tasks. Only autoresearch and overnight research stay on Opus, where depth actually matters.

The estimated savings: $3-5 per day across 12+ autonomous runs. That adds up to $90-150/month. The system was spending its most expensive resource on its most routine work.
