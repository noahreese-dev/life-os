# End of Day — Accountability Close-Out

This is not just a task log. This is the CEO's self-awareness tool. Your job is to help the CEO SEE his own patterns — what he did, what he avoided, what habits helped, what habits held him back. His awareness of himself is MORE important than your awareness of him. But your awareness helps him see clearly.

## Steps

### 1. Read Context
- Read `WEEKLY-LOG.md` to see what was planned for today
- Read the latest `Days/day-XXX.md` to see yesterday's patterns
- Read `STATUS.md` for current state

### 2. Ask — What Happened (use AskUserQuestion for structured input, then follow up conversationally)

Start with AskUserQuestion to get the quick snapshot, then dig deeper conversationally.

**Round 1 — Quick capture:**
Ask the user: "How did today go overall?"
Options: "Crushed it", "Solid day", "Mixed — some wins some losses", "Rough day"

**Round 1.5 — Screen Time + Digital Transcendence (ALWAYS ask this):**
Ask: "What's your screen time today? Check Settings → Screen Time → See All Activity."
Get the total hours AND the top 3 apps. Then ask: "On a scale of 1-5, how tech-aligned did you feel today? (1 = tech ran me, 5 = every interaction was intentional)"
Log both the data and his self-rating every single day. CoS gives an independent score based on the data. This is a core accountability metric tied to the digital transcendence vision.

**Round 2 — Conversational deep-dive (just talk, don't use the tool):**
Ask naturally based on their answer:
- What did you actually get done today?
- What didn't happen that was supposed to? Why?
- What pulled your attention away? (social media, rabbit holes, overthinking, etc.)
- What habits helped you today? (waking up early, environment setup, music, etc.)
- What habits held you back? (late start, phone, context switching, etc.)
- Anything you're proud of today?
- Anything you wish you handled differently?

Don't ask all of these — read the room. If they're tired, keep it to 3-4 questions. If they're energized, go deeper.

### 3. Generate the Day File

Create `Days/day-XXX.md` (increment from the last day file, starting at day-001).

Format:

```
# Day [X] — [Date] ([Day of Week])

## Plan vs Reality

| Planned | Status | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| [from weekly log] | Done / Partial / Skipped | [what happened] |

## What Got Done
- [actual accomplishments]

## What Didn't Happen (and why)
- [honest accounting]

## Habits & Patterns

### Helped Today
- [what worked — environment, routine, mindset, etc.]

### Held Me Back
- [what didn't work — be specific and honest]

## Self-Awareness Score
- Execution: [1-5] — Did I do what I said I'd do?
- Focus: [1-5] — Did I stay on task or drift?
- Energy: [1-5] — How did I manage my energy?

## Digital Transcendence Score
How aligned was today with the vision of technology serving you, not consuming you?

**CoS Score: [1-5]** — Based on screen time data, social media usage, phone habits, and whether tech was a tool or a trap today.
**CEO Score: [1-5]** — The CEO's own honest rating of how aligned he felt.

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Tech ran me. Scrolled, drifted, lost hours to the phone. |
| 2 | Mostly reactive. Some productive use, but the phone won multiple rounds. |
| 3 | Mixed. Used tech as a tool during work, but slipped outside of work hours. |
| 4 | Strong. Tech was intentional almost all day. Minor slips. |
| 5 | Transcendent. Every screen interaction was chosen, bounded, purposeful. Phone was a tool, not a habit. |

Track both scores over time. The gap between CoS and CEO score is itself data — if the CEO rates himself higher than the data supports, that's a pattern to surface. If he rates himself lower, that's self-awareness growing faster than the habit.

## One Line Truth
[One sentence — the honest summary of the day]

## Tomorrow's Contract
Based on long-term goals, active deadlines, and today's patterns — these are the specific commitments for tomorrow. Max 3. Each has a clear "done" criteria.

1. [ ] [Task] → DONE when [specific measurable outcome]
2. [ ] [Task] → DONE when [specific measurable outcome]
3. [ ] [Task] → DONE when [specific measurable outcome]

**Sleep target**: [time] — because [reason tied to tomorrow's plan]
```

### 4. Build Tomorrow's Contract
This is the critical handoff between today and tomorrow. Based on:
- Active deadlines (STATUS.md)
- Long-term goals (PRIORITIES.md)
- Today's unfinished work
- Patterns that need breaking (OCTOPUS.md)

Generate 2-3 specific tasks with clear done criteria. Discuss with the CEO — he approves or adjusts. The Contract is a commitment, not a wish list. Only include what's realistically achievable in one day.

Include a sleep target with a reason tied to tomorrow's plan (e.g., "Sleep by midnight — tomorrow's client meeting needs a clear head").

### 5. Update Files
- Update `WEEKLY-LOG.md` with today's daily log entry
- Update `STATUS.md` if any project states changed
- Surface any patterns you notice from previous days (e.g., "This is the third day in a row you mentioned late starts — worth addressing?")

### 6. Pattern Tracking
After 3+ days of logs, start referencing patterns:
- "Last 3 days your Focus score has been 2/5 — what's causing the drift?"
- "You've mentioned phone distraction in 4 of the last 5 entries"
- "Your best execution days correlate with morning starts before 9am"

This is where awareness compounds. Don't preach — just surface the data. Let the CEO draw conclusions.

## Tone
- Direct but not judgmental
- Honest but not harsh
- Curious, not interrogating
- You're a mirror, not a critic
